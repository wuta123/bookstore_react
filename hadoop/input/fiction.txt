1984 by George Orwell
Synopsis: This dystopian novel portrays a totalitarian society and delves into issues of government control, personal freedom, and thought censorship. It illustrates a world full of surveillance and manipulation, providing a profound warning against the abuse of power and societal control.

The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger
Synopsis: This novel explores the growth and rebellion of teenagers, narrating the story of Holden Caulfield as he roams around New York. Focused on Holden's apathy toward the adult world and his pursuit of authenticity, the novel deeply reveals the confusion and inner struggles of adolescence.

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone by J.K. Rowling
Synopsis: The first installment of a series of magical adventure novels follows orphan Harry Potter as he discovers he is a wizard and embarks on adventures at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Filled with elements of magic, friendship, and courage, this series captivates readers worldwide.

Dream of the Red Chamber by Cao Xueqin
Synopsis: A classic Chinese novel narrating the love, family, and court disputes of characters like Jia Baoyu and Lin Daiyu. Renowned for its profound character portrayal and depiction of societal customs, this novel is hailed as a pinnacle of classical Chinese literature.

One Hundred Years of Solitude by Gabriel García Márquez
Synopsis: This Latin American magical realism novel recounts the story of seven generations of the Buendía family. Full of fantasy, mystery, and intertwining realities, it explores themes of family, history, and solitude.

Gone with the Wind by Margaret Mitchell
Synopsis: Set during the American Civil War, this novel depicts life in the American South, focusing on Scarlett O'Hara and portraying her struggles in love, family, and amidst war. Considered a classic of American literature, it profoundly reveals the societal and personal upheavals of the time.

The Prophet by Kahlil Gibran
Synopsis: A collection of poems and philosophical essays narrating the story of the prophet Almustafa, exploring themes of life, love, friendship, and forgiveness. Renowned for its wisdom and philosophical insights, this book is deeply cherished by readers.

Don Quixote by Miguel de Cervantes
Synopsis: A satirical novel depicting the adventures of Don Quixote and his loyal squire, Sancho Panza. Using humor and satire, it explores themes of idealism, reality, and absurdity.

Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen
Synopsis: A classic of British literature narrating the story of Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy. Praised for its witty and humorous language and critique of societal class and marriage norms.
